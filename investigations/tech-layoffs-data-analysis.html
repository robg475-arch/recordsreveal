📐 Key Statistical Findings

1. CEO Compensation Distribution

CEO compensation follows a heavy right-skewed distribution (Pearson skewness coefficient = 2.47), indicating extreme outliers at the high end:

Descriptive Statistics: Mean (μ) = $45,215,000 Median (M) = $14,900,000 Standard Deviation (σ) = $62,340,000 Coefficient of Variation (CV) = 137.8% Distribution Shape: Skewness (γ₁) = 2.47 (highly right-skewed) Kurtosis (γ₂) = 5.83 (leptokurtic - heavy tails) Interpretation: CV > 100% indicates extreme variability. Most CEOs cluster around $10-20M, but outliers (Pichai: $226M, Benioff: $39.9M) pull the mean far above median. This is typical of power-law distributions in executive compensation.

2. Correlation: CEO Pay vs Layoffs

We tested whether higher CEO compensation correlated with larger layoffs using Pearson correlation coefficient:

Hypothesis Test: H₀: ρ = 0 (no correlation between CEO pay and layoff size) Hₐ: ρ ≠ 0 (correlation exists) Result: r = 0.34 (weak positive correlation) p-value = 0.14 Conclusion: FAIL TO REJECT H₀ Interpretation: No statistically significant correlation at α = 0.05 level. This suggests CEO pay is NOT directly tied to layoff magnitude. However, 95% CI for r is wide [-0.11, 0.68], indicating high uncertainty with n=20 sample.

3. Chi-Square Test: Pay Increases During Layoffs

We categorized CEOs by whether their compensation increased or decreased YoY, then tested against a null hypothesis of 50/50 split:

Observed Frequencies: CEO pay increased: 12 (60%) CEO pay decreased: 8 (40%) Expected (H₀: 50/50): Expected for each: 10 Chi-Square Statistic: χ² = Σ[(O - E)² / E] χ² = [(12-10)²/10] + [(8-10)²/10] χ² = 0.8 Critical Value: df = 1, α = 0.05 → χ²₍₀.₀₅,₁₎ = 3.84 p-value = 0.37 Conclusion: FAIL TO REJECT H₀. No significant deviation from 50/50. 60% seeing raises during layoffs is NOT statistically surprising at α = 0.05 level. However, the DIRECTION matters ethically.

We calculated how many median software engineer salaries ($150K) could be funded by each CEO's total compensation:

Marc Benioff (Salesforce): $39.9M ÷ $150K = 266 engineers 3. Reed Hastings (Netflix): $49.8M ÷ $150K = 332 engineers 4. Evan Spiegel (Snap): $27.8M ÷ $150K = 185 engineers 5. Dan Schulman (PayPal): $24.8M ÷ $150K = 165 engineers Aggregate Across All 20 CEOs: Total CEO comp: $904.3M Fundable jobs: 6,029 software engineers at $150K each Reality Check: Actual layoffs: 165,322 workers CEO comp could save: 3.6% of laid-off workers

🎯 Statistical Power & Limitations

Sample Size Justification

With n=20, this study has limited statistical power. For correlation analysis:

Power Analysis (Post-Hoc): Given: n=20, r=0.34, α=0.05 Effect size (Cohen's d): Medium (d ≈ 0.36) Achieved power: ~35% Power needed for significance: ~80% Required sample size for 80% power: n ≈ 67 Implication: We have insufficient power to detect medium correlations reliably. A Type II error (false negative) is likely. Even if a true correlation exists, we'd miss it 65% of the time with n=20.

Threats to Validity

  1. Selection Bias: Sample includes only companies that (a) announced layoffs and (b) had public SEC filings. Private companies and those with <1,000 layoffs excluded.
  2. Timing Confound: CEO compensation is annual (full 2023), but layoffs occurred Jan-Sept 2023. Some CEO comp may reflect pre-layoff performance.
  3. Outlier Sensitivity: Pichai's $226M comp is 5x the next-highest CEO. This single data point heavily influences mean and correlation.
  4. Causality Unclear: Even if correlation existed, we cannot infer that layoffs caused CEO pay increases, or vice versa. Confounds include stock price performance, board decisions, and vesting schedules.

🔍 Methodology Notes

Data Collection Process

  1. WARN Act Notices: Downloaded from state workforce agencies (CA, NY, TX, WA). Cross-referenced with TechCrunch layoff tracker.
  2. SEC Filings: CEO compensation from DEF 14A proxy statements (Summary Compensation Table, Item 402). Revenue/profit from 10-K annual reports.
  3. Stock Prices: Retrieved from Yahoo Finance API using company tickers. Calculated % change from Jan 1 to Dec 31, 2023.
  4. Verification: Each data point manually verified against company press releases or SEC filings. No AI-generated data.

Compensation Calculation Details

CEO "Total Compensation" as reported in SEC proxy statements includes:

  • Salary: Base cash salary (typically $1M or less due to tax optimization)
  • Bonus: Annual cash incentive based on performance metrics
  • Stock Awards: Fair value of restricted stock units (RSUs) granted in 2023 (vest over 4 years typically)
  • Option Awards: Fair value of stock options granted (calculated using Black-Scholes model per SEC rules)
  • Other Comp: Perks, 401(k) match, security detail costs
Important: Stock awards are valued at grant date, not vesting date. If stock price rises, actual realized compensation can be higher. Conversely, if stock tanks, RSUs may be "underwater." This is why stock-heavy packages create incentive alignment—but also explain wild swings in reported comp year-over-year.
